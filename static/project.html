Number of Wildfires over the Years (Cause Separated)

The amount of wildfires in California are increasing each year and continue to destroy thousands of homes. To better prevent the occurrence and spread of wildfires, many researches have been conducted by scientists to investigate the relationship between global warming and the drastically increasing amount of wildfires. However, there is still debate within the scientific community on whether global warming has a direct impact upon the rise of wildfires. As the supporters claim, global warming has caused rising temperatures and more droughts, and thus, prolonged Californiaâ€™s drought season by at least 2 and half months longer. The National Geographic also agrees that the higher air temperature causing the vegetation to be significantly drier and more prone to wildfires. On the other hand, opponents, including two professors at UCLA, claim that there have been fewer and fewer wildfires in California since its peak in 1980. Our project intends to utilize data science and data visualization to investigate whether rising temperatures caused by global warming increases wildfires over time. The issue of wildfires in California has caught national attention and there has been a large amount of data collected related to the cause and magnitude of wildfires. Furthermore, many related projects have been done in the past to raise awareness on the issue. For example, the WiFireLab at the San Diego SuperComputer Center created a software program, FireMap, to predict where the fire would spread since 2017 using weather data and locations of wildfires happened in the past. The data and the program were used by many departments in California, and helped firefighters to inform the public about potential evacuations. In addition, data scientists have also visualized the wildfire season to provide a better visual representation of the wildfires in California throughout the year.
